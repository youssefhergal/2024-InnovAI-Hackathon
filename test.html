Working with Real Numbers and the Number Line

This chapter reviews different types of numbers and shows how they are represented on a number line, prepar ing students for future algebraic operations involving numbers. ➡️

Types of numbers (integers, rational, irrational)

This section explores the different categories of real numbers:

  • Integers: Whole numbers and their negatives (..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...)
  • Rational Numbers: Numbers that can be expressed as a fraction p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0. This includes terminating and repeating decimals.
  • Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers. Their decimal representations are non-terminating and non-repeating (e.g., π, √2).

All these types of numbers together form the set of real numbers.

Key Point:

Understanding the classification of numbers is fundamental for later mathematical operations.

Number line representation

The number line is a visual representation of real numbers. Zero is at the center, positive numbers are to the right, and negative numbers are to the left. Each point on the line corresponds to a unique real number.

Key Point:

The number line provides a visual way to compare and order numbers.

Ordering numbers

Numbers on the number line are ordered. Numbers to the right are greater than numbers to the left. We use inequality symbols (<, >, ≤, ≥) to compare numbers.

Key Point:

The number line helps visualize the order of real numbers.

5 > 2  (5 is greater than 2)-3 < 1  (-3 is less than 1)-1 ≤ 0  (-1 is less than or equal to 0)

Absolute value

The absolute value of a number is its distance from zero on the number line. It is always non-negative. The symbol for absolute value is | |. For example, |3| = 3 and |-3| = 3.

Key Point:

Absolute value represents distance from zero, disregarding the sign.

|7| = 7|-5| = 5|0| = 0

Comparing numbers

Numbers can be compared using the number line or by directly comparing their values. Remember to consider both the magnitude and the sign when comparing negative numbers.

Key Point:

Accurate comparison of numbers requires attention to both magnitude and sign.
